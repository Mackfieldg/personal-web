Personal History & Academic Journey

Early Life & Education

I was born on 3 June, 2003 in Lilongwe, Malawi, to my parents Mr and Mrs George Kadyaula. My early years were shaped by a curious environment that encouraged exploration, leading me to Tsogolo Education Centre, then later to Mdzobwe Full Primary School for my foundational education. I did very well on my PSLC and was selected to one of the country's top secondary schools, Mzuzu Government Secondary School. There I first began to appreciate the intersection of different disciplines and developed an interest in how the world operates. My form four MSCE exam results got me selected to the top university in the country to pursue my undergraduate degree, Bachelor of Science in Biology.

University & Personal Growth

University was a transformative period that allowed me to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application. While my core curriculum focused on biology, I spent elective time and personal hours diving into subjects that also fascinate me.

Areas of Interest & Intellectual Pursuit

My free time during holidays and weekends was used to learn programming and data analysis where I learned the power of code to solve problems and the ability of data to reveal hidden patterns in biological or social systems. Though not my major-related discipline, the humanities hold a strong interest for me. To balance the technical, I am deeply invested in philosophy, geopolitics, and literature.

Future Aspirations

My ultimate goal is to synthesize these diverse interests by pursuing an MSc in Bioinformatics. I believe the future of medicine and biological discovery lies at the intersection of computational power and laboratory expertise. By combining my passion for programming with my foundation in lab sciences, I aim to contribute to the field of genomics and personalized medicine.
